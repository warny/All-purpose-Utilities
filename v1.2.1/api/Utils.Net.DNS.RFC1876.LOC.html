Remarks

The binary format of the LOC record is defined as follows (each field is one or more octets): 

   MSB                                           LSB
   +--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+
 0 |        VERSION        |         SIZE          |
   +--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+
 2 |       HORIZ PRE       |       VERT PRE        |
   +--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+
 4 |                   LATITUDE                    |
   +--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+
 6 |                   LATITUDE                    |
   +--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+
 8 |                   LONGITUDE                   |
   +--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+
10 |                   LONGITUDE                   |
   +--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+
12 |                   ALTITUDE                    |
   +--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+
14 |                   ALTITUDE                    |
   +--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+--+
 (octet)

The fields are defined as:

  • VERSION: A one-octet version number (must be zero).
  • SIZE: The diameter of a sphere enclosing the described entity, in centimeters. It is represented as a pair of four-bit unsigned integers: the first (most significant) is the base, and the second is the exponent (power of 10). For example, 0x15 represents 1e5 cm.
  • HORIZ PRE: The horizontal precision of the location (i.e., the diameter of the horizontal "circle of error"), expressed in centimeters with the same exponential representation as SIZE. To obtain the value in meters, divide by 100.
  • VERT PRE: The vertical precision, representing the total potential vertical error, expressed in centimeters using the same exponential representation. To convert to meters, divide by 100.
  • LATITUDE: The latitude of the location, stored as a 32-bit integer in network byte order, representing thousandths of a second of arc. A value of 2^31 indicates the equator; values above that are north.
  • LONGITUDE: The longitude of the location, stored as a 32-bit integer in network byte order, representing thousandths of a second of arc, rounded away from the prime meridian (2^31). Values above 2^31 are east.
  • ALTITUDE: The altitude of the location, stored as a 32-bit integer in centimeters, with an offset such that a value of altitudeZeroCorrection represents 0 meters relative to the WGS 84 spheroid. To convert to meters, divide by 100.

The class also provides properties that convert the exponential representation into standard double values (e.g., meters for precision, seconds of arc for latitude/longitude, etc.), and vice versa.
